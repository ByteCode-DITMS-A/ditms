B.C.S(B.Sc.Computer Science)

B.C.S. is acronym of Bachelor of Computer Science. This degree course is affiliated to Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Marathwada University, Aurangabad.

Scope

After completion of B.C.S. the students can join any software industry or can seek admission for computer P.G. Courses like M. C. S., M.Sc. (Comp. Sci. & Information Technology),M.C.A., M.C.M. and M.B.A..

Eligibility

The candidates who have Passed 10+2 and scored 45% HSC Examination in Science Stream are eligible for this course. Admission to this three- year degree course is strictly on merit basis, based on objective type questions followed by group discussion and personal interviews.

Registration

Prospective candidates may register on payment of the following.

Prospectus ₹50

Documents

In order to take admission, respective documents are as follow:

  • 10th Marksheet (only Xerox 2 copies)
  • 12th Marksheet (Original + 3 xerox copies)
  • Leaving Certificate of last school/college (3 copies)
  • Migration Certificate (For students coming from other States) (3 copies)
  • Caste Certificate

Admission

The candidates with 45% or above marks will be registered and admitted to this course as per their merit position. The intake capacity of the course may vary. Every students have to fulfill admission procedure by paying requisite fees and submitting the original documents before due date or else the next candidate in the waiting list will be admitted.

Fee Structure

The fees structure may vary.

Programme Outcomes (POs)

  • PO-1: To develop problem solving abilities using a computer.
  • PO-2: To prepare necessary knowledge base for R&D in computer science.
  • PO-3: To build the necessary skill set and analytical abilities for developing computer based solutions for real life problems.
  • PO-4: To train students in professional skills related to software industry.
  • PO-5: To imbibe quality software development in computer science.

Program Specific Outcomes (PSO)

  • PSO-1: To help students build up a successful career in computer science.
  • PSO-2: Apply computer science theory and software development fundamentals to produce computing based solutions.
  • PSO-3: Recognize professional responsibilities and make informed judgments in computing practice based on legal and ethical principles.
  • PSO-4: Communicate properly in a variety of professional contexts.
  • PSO-5: To develop an understanding of professional, ethical, legal, security and social issues and responsibilities for the computing profession.
